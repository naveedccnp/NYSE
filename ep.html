§0 · EP Screening Criteria — What to Look For
📈 Earnings & Revenue (Core)
Beats revenue OR going to beat & higher than street consensus
Earnings higher or going to be higher than street consensus
Going forward revenues higher than consensus — next quarter & the year
Going forward earnings better than the street — next Q & for the year
Margins increase above consensus (gross, operating, or net)
📊 Technical Qualifiers
Price above $20 — cheaper stocks move less
More than 1 million shares average daily volume
High volume before the open
Ready to make a new high, or break through a high for a significant period
Fits an identifiable pattern (e.g. stocks up on open that just go higher this season)
Reported yesterday AMC & trading higher premarket than yesterday's AH close
🔴 Short Squeeze Setup
Heavily shorted — more than 20% short interest is key threshold
Sold off a lot lately — beaten down stock starting to move back up
News that made it go down confirmed exaggerated or untrue
Tier I bank defends the stock (Goldman, Morgan Stanley, Citi)
🔬 Catalyst & Story
New product to sell that is unexpected
Gaining market share
Biotech: Phase III data (most important), Phase II also qualifies
Potential takeover candidate — rumor circulating. Check sector peers too.
Government investigation — be careful on the long side
🏦 Analyst Upgrades
Tier I — Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Citigroup carry the most weight
More than one upgrade in a stock is a stronger signal
Pay special attention to price target given vs. where stock is currently trading
Tier II (JPM, BofA, Wells, UBS) and Tier III count — just less weight
⚠ Disqualifiers & Risk Flags
Price below $20 — reduces move potential significantly
Average daily volume below 1M shares
Government investigation — use extra caution on long side
Biotech Phase I only — very early stage, high risk
Only one Tier III upgrade — weakest signal
